About the Place

The Dewart Building is a beautifully maintained, historic building loaded with charm. Unlike other downtown office spaces that tend to be narrow and deep, The Dewart Building allows for a wider range of configurations, perfect for all types of businesses, arts and creative uses. The antique mail chute, brass railing and life sized Blues Brothers statues are among the unique characteristics found in this building.

Being on upper State Street, there are sweeping views of downtown New London from the upper floors of The Dewart. We are located right across from the Garde Theatre and The Mohican and next to the historic New London Public Library. There are also numerous restaurants within walking distance, making lunchtime a convenient part of your day. We are also only a few blocks from New London’s transportation hub. Trains, buses, ferries, we have it all right here in historic downtown New London. It’s a great place to work and play!

History of Dewart building

The  Dewart Building at 300 State Street New London was originally The Plant Building, built in 1914 for Morton Plant (1852-1918) He was a millionaire financier who invested substantially in the development of New London. The building contained shops on the first floor with offices and a large assembly hall above. It was designed by architect Dudley St. Clare Donnelly, who has his own offices on the fourth floor. The Plant Building was later renamed for William J. Dewart, the local business manager for Frank Munsey, the magazine publisher who built the Mohican Hotel across the street from The Dewart Building.
